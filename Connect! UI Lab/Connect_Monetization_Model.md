# Connect! — Monetization Model

> **Status:** Working draft for review. NOT yet merged into CLAUDE.md.
> **Context:** Companion to the Community-First / running-led pivot. Read alongside §1 (Product) and the Stage plan.
> **One line:** Connect! is not a transaction-commission business — it is an **audience / community layer**. Runners participate free; **brands, employers, and serious organizers pay** to reach and serve them.

---

## 0. The core principle (read this first)

**Decouple who you acquire from who you bill.**

- **Who you acquire:** runners / members — *free, forever, by design.*
- **Who pays you:** whoever has a budget to **reach** or **serve** that audience.

Runners are your **inventory and audience**, not your customers. The classic mistake is trying to make the audience pay — in a free sport that kills the community and the growth. Free participation is the *engine*: it grows the audience, which is exactly what makes the audience valuable to the people who do pay. This is the media / community playbook, not the marketplace-commission playbook.

**Why this is a different (and fatter) game than Playtomic**

| | Playtomic | Connect! |
|---|---|---|
| Monetizes | Transactions on **bookable courts** (per-booking commission + venue SaaS) | **Attention, belonging, and data** of an affluent active community |
| Margin shape | Thin, per-transaction (~€0.20/booking) | Recurring B2B deals (4–5 figures), asset-light |
| Asset dependency | Needs courts / venue inventory | None — rides existing offline communities |
| Reachable in running? | No (no court = nothing to book) | Yes — running is the wedge |

Playtomic owns the court-booking transaction layer. It structurally cannot monetize a free Corniche run — there is nothing there to take a cut of. That layer was never going to monetize a *running* audience anyway. Connect! monetizes a layer Playtomic does not operate in.

## 1. Who is free vs. who pays

| Group | Free? | Role |
|---|---|---|
| Runners / members | Free | The audience (inventory) |
| Casual organizers | Free | Supply / acquisition channel |
| **Brands & sponsors** | **Pay** | Want to reach the audience |
| **Employers (corporate wellness)** | **Pay** | Want to serve their staff |
| **Serious organizers** | **Pay (SaaS)** | Want pro tooling for paid events |
| Members buying premium experiences | **Pay per item** | Pay for *experiences*, never to participate |

## 2. Revenue lines (ranked by near-term fit)

### Line 1 — Brand sponsorship & partnerships  *(primary, recurring B2B)*
**Who pays:** sportswear/footwear (New Balance already runs a Doha club; Nike, On, Hoka want run-club presence); specialty coffee & F&B (the café loop); banks & telcos (QNB, Ooredoo — large "active-living" CSR budgets tied to Qatar National Vision 2030 / health agenda); real estate & destinations (Msheireb, The Pearl, Lusail — want footfall + lifestyle association).
**What they pay for:** branded run series, "official home" status, event presence, and **audience data / ROI reports**.
**Why Playtomic doesn't own it:** Playtomic sells court bookings, not community sponsorship of non-court sports.
**What it needs from you:** audience scale + credibility, branded event pages, and the **Sponsorship Report Builder** (already in your feature list — it is the *sales weapon* for this line).
**Phase:** 1 (local/café/sportswear) → 2 (banks, telcos, regional).

### Line 2 — Corporate wellness / B2B employee programs  *(strong EARLY line — scale-independent)*
**Who pays:** companies, for staff run clubs, step/distance challenges, inter-company events, wellness reporting.
**Why it's the smart early wedge:** a corporate deal needs **one decision-maker (HR/wellness lead)** and **only that company's own employees** — it does **not** require a city-wide audience. So you can earn revenue *before* you have mass scale. Recurring budget. GCC corporates have real wellness spend.
**What it needs from you:** company/team grouping, private company clubs, a challenge/leaderboard, and an **admin view + participation report** for the sponsor.
**Phase:** 1 (can be your *first* paid line).

### Line 3 — Paid premium experiences & events
**Who pays:** members, per experience. Free weekly runs are the funnel; **paid experiences** monetize.
**Examples:** destination/desert sunrise run + breakfast, ladies-only weekend retreat, **Ramadan night-run series**, race-day packages, branded merch.
**What it needs from you:** event pages + ticketing via an **external payment rail** (see Risks — do not build/hold funds).
**Phase:** 1–2.

### Line 4 — Organizer SaaS (premium tier)  *("arm the dealer")*
**Who pays:** serious organizers running paid events/training camps.
**What they pay for:** registration + waitlist, **reconciliation**, **sponsor-report generator**, member CRM, branded event pages.
**Why Playtomic doesn't own it:** Playtomic Manager serves racket **venues**; nobody serves community/endurance **organizers**.
**Phase:** 2.

### Line 5 — Later / optional
Coaching & training-plan marketplace (reintroduces payment rails — defer); gear affiliate / commerce (low margin — secondary).

## 3. Phased rollout (revenue tied to scale)

| Phase | Timeframe* | Goal | Revenue |
|---|---|---|---|
| **0 — Doha: build audience** | ~0–6 mo | Land 1–2 hero organizers; prove the playbook; café loop as **barter** (foot traffic + install driver) | **~$0 by design** |
| **1 — Doha: first revenue** | ~6–18 mo | Corporate wellness deals; café "official home" turns paid (report converts it); first local/sportswear sponsor; paid premium events; SaaS for the serious few | First recurring B2B |
| **2 — GCC (UAE / Saudi): lucrative** | ~18 mo+ | Regional brand deals, multi-city corporate programs, scaled event/sponsor packages | **Where it becomes a real business** |

\*Indicative for a solo builder; gate each phase on proof, not the calendar.

**The expansion link (important):** single-city Doha's sponsorship pool is thin. The model becomes lucrative at **GCC scale** because brands and corporates buy **region-wide reach**. Monetization and the Qatar → UAE → Saudi expansion are **one plan, not two**. (Caveat: community products re-cold-start in every city — you replicate the *playbook*, not the audience.)

## 6. What this requires in the product (→ implications for CLAUDE.md)

- **Sponsorship Report Builder** — aggregate audience metrics & demographics. **PDPPL-safe: aggregate / consented only; never sell personal data.**
- **Branded event pages + club↔sponsor linkage** ("official home of [club]").
- **Corporate/team layer** — private company clubs, challenges, sponsor admin view + reporting.
- **Paid events** — integrate an **external ticketing / payment provider**; do **not** build or hold funds (keeps you out of the compliance you deliberately avoided).
- **Organizer SaaS paywall tier** — gate reconciliation, report generator, CRM.
- **Retention primitives** — perk network, member identity/stats, cross-club discovery.

## 7. Honest risks

- **Delayed revenue.** Phase 0 ≈ $0. Mitigated by low burn (no payments, solo) — but no early cash; needs patience/runway.
- **Chicken-and-egg.** Sponsorship needs audience scale. **Mitigate:** lead with **corporate wellness** (scale-independent) + **café barter** early.
- **PDPPL (enforced; penalties QAR 1–5M).** Any data monetization must be **aggregate & consented**; the report must be privacy-clean.
- **Payment rails.** Paid events/coaching reintroduce compliance — **outsource the rail**, don't hold funds.
- **Gulf sponsorship is relationship-driven and lumpy.** Early concentration risk (1–2 big sponsors = fragile).
- **Single-city ceiling.** May never be big without GCC expansion — and each new city is a fresh cold start.

---

## 8. Summary

You are building **the audience layer for active lifestyle in the Gulf** — a high-value, brand-coveted, hard-to-reach community — and monetizing it the way media and communities do: **brands and employers pay to reach and serve the audience; organizers pay for tooling; members pay only for premium experiences; everyone runs for free.** That is a different category from Playtomic's thin per-booking commission — fatter margin, asset-light, and structurally outside Playtomic's reach. The pieces you already designed (Sponsorship Report Builder, café loop, share card) are not a backup plan after commissions — **they are the main model.**
